## 📘 NIST SP 800-61r2 — Computer Security Incident Handling Guide

### 🔹 Overview
- A guideline by NIST for handling cybersecurity incidents.
- Focuses on building an effective **incident response capability**.
- Applies to organizations of all sizes and sectors.

---

### 🔹 Purpose
- Provide a structured approach to:
  - Detect incidents
  - Analyze threats
  - Respond effectively
  - Recover systems quickly
- Minimize damage and reduce recovery time.

---

### 🔹 Incident Response Lifecycle

1. **Preparation**
   - Develop policies, procedures, and response plans.
   - Train staff and establish an incident response team.
   - Deploy tools (e.g., monitoring, logging, SIEM).

2. **Detection & Analysis**
   - Identify potential security incidents through alerts and monitoring.
   - Validate and analyze incidents.
   - Determine scope, impact, and type.

3. **Containment, Eradication & Recovery**
   - Contain the incident to limit damage.
   - Remove the root cause (e.g., malware, compromised accounts).
   - Restore systems and ensure they are secure.

4. **Post-Incident Activity**
   - Conduct lessons learned sessions.
   - Improve policies, tools, and defenses.
   - Document findings for future reference.

---

### 🔹 Incident Response Team (CSIRT)
- A dedicated team responsible for handling incidents.
- Common structures:
  - Centralized
  - Distributed
  - Coordinating team
- Key roles:
  - Incident analysis
  - Communication
  - Documentation and reporting

---

### 🔹 Incident Classification & Prioritization
- Categorize incidents (e.g., malware, unauthorized access, DoS).
- Prioritize based on:
  - Impact
  - Urgency
  - Scope
- Helps allocate resources effectively.

---

### 🔹 Communication & Coordination
- Essential for effective incident handling.
- Involves:
  - Internal teams (IT, management, legal)
  - External parties (vendors, law enforcement)
- Requires clear reporting and escalation procedures.

---

### 🔹 Tools & Techniques
- Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
- Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
- Log analysis tools
- Forensics and monitoring tools

---

### 🔹 Best Practices
- Maintain and regularly update incident response plans.
- Enable continuous monitoring and logging.
- Train staff and run simulations.
- Share threat intelligence when possible.
- Integrate incident response with risk management.

---

### 🔹 Importance
- Critical for minimizing the impact of cyber incidents.
- Improves organizational resilience and security posture.
- Supports faster recovery and better future preparedness.
