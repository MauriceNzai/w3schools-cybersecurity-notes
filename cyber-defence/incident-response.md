## Cybersecurity Incident Response

### What is an Incident
An incident is any adverse event or potential threat to computer systems or networks that may cause harm to an organization. Not all incidents require escalation, but those with significant impact are handled by an Incident Response Team (IRT).

### Incident Response Team (IRT)
An IRT is a dedicated group responsible for handling security incidents. It may include:
- Cybersecurity specialists
- Security Operations (SOC)
- IT and Network Operations
- Developers
- Legal and HR teams

The goal of the IRT is to minimize damage, prevent attacker movement, and align response efforts with business objectives.

### PICERL Methodology (NIST SP 800-61)
Incident response follows a flexible 6-phase lifecycle:

1. **Preparation**
   - Develop playbooks, procedures, and rules of engagement
   - Ensure access to logs, tools, and communication channels
   - Train teams and maintain readiness

2. **Identification**
   - Detect and classify incidents using alerts (SIEM, IDS/IPS, EDR) or user reports
   - Assess scope, impact, affected systems, and compromised credentials

3. **Containment**
   - Limit damage and stop attackers
   - Actions include isolating systems, blocking access, changing passwords
   - Preserve evidence (disk images, memory dumps)

4. **Eradication**
   - Remove attacker artifacts
   - Methods include restoring from backups or rebuilding systems

5. **Recovery**
   - Restore systems to normal operation
   - Monitor for reinfection or missed threats

6. **Lessons Learned**
   - Analyze the incident response process
   - Identify gaps in tools, logs, and procedures
   - Produce reports and improve future defenses

### Key Takeaways
- Incident response is a continuous and iterative process.
- Preparation and coordination across teams are critical.
- Effective response minimizes damage and improves organizational resilience.
