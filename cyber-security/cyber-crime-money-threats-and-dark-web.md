# Cybersecurity Notes — Cyber Crime, Money Threats & Dark Web

## 1. Cyber Crime

### Definition
Cyber crime refers to illegal activities carried out using computers or the internet.

### Common Types
- Identity theft  
- Online predators  
- Business Email Compromise (BEC)  
- Ransomware attacks  
- Theft of intellectual property  

### Why Cyber Crime is Increasing
- Easy to execute  
- Low risk of being caught  
- High financial rewards  
- Ability to target many victims at once  
- Use of cryptocurrencies for anonymity  

### Money Laundering Techniques
- Use of cryptocurrencies (e.g., Bitcoin)  
- Tumblers/mixers to hide transaction trails  
- Splitting transactions across multiple accounts  

### Types of Cyber Criminals
- State-sponsored attackers  
- Organized hacking groups  
- Individual hackers  

### Challenges in Catching Criminals
- Easy to hide identity  
- Use of international infrastructure  
- Legal and jurisdiction issues  


## 2. Money-Making Threats

### Overview
Cybercrime is often driven by financial gain.

### Common Financial Threats
- Ransomware (demanding payment from victims)  
- Phishing and online scams  
- Selling stolen data (credentials, credit cards)  
- Identity theft and fraud  
- Cryptojacking (unauthorized crypto mining)  

### Role of Cryptocurrencies
- Enable anonymous transactions  
- Harder to trace than traditional banking  
- Widely used in cybercrime  

### Underground Economy
- Buying and selling stolen data  
- Trading hacking tools and services  
- Organized cybercrime marketplaces  


## 3. The Dark Web

### Definition
The Dark Web is a hidden part of the internet that requires special software to access.

### Key Characteristics
- Strong anonymity and encryption  
- Uses special domains (e.g., .onion)  
- Traffic routed through multiple servers  

### Access
- Requires tools like Tor Browser  
- Supports anonymous browsing and communication  

### Uses
- Privacy-focused communication  
- Hidden online services  
- Both legal and illegal activities  

### Risks and Criminal Activity
- Illegal marketplaces (drugs, weapons, stolen data)  
- Financial fraud and scams  
- Sale of hacking tools and services  
- Difficult to trace users  

### Safety Note
- Accessing the Dark Web can expose users to risks and illegal content
